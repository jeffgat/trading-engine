Where the Council Agrees

All five advisors agree the .shift(1) construction is not lookahead. Yesterday's daily close is known before the 09:30 NY open. There is no temporal violation in the feature construction.

There is also consensus that the gate is mechanically implementable: a morning batch job, 25-bar rolling close history, two frozen thresholds, and a regime lookup.

Where the Council Clashes

The real disagreement: are the vol thresholds a form of data leakage?

The Outsider (supported by 4/5 peer reviewers) says yes: thresholds frozen at 12.52%/20.40% were derived from the full pre-holdout window (2016-2024). Any backtest within that window used the full distribution to set its own cutoffs — "distributional lookahead" or "threshold snooping."

First Principles, Expansionist, and Executor treat this as a non-issue: the thresholds are fixed constants, computed once, applied forward.

The Contrarian's midnight-ET boundary concern is legitimate operationally (corroborated by the codebase's own MEMORY.md), but reviewers note it is engine-wide, not gate-specific.

Blind Spots the Council Caught

  • Post-hoc vs. pre-trade filtering: The backtest filters already-simulated trades. Live execution suppresses orders before placement. Trade sequencing (daily limits, position state) differs between the two — the backtest may undercount available trades on good-regime days.
  • Holiday edge case: After a market holiday, .shift(1) lands on a non-trading day (NaN). The classifier's behavior on this edge case is unspecified.
  • WF threshold shortcut: Walk-forward folds use globally-frozen thresholds, not fold-by-fold recomputed thresholds. Sound for holdout confirmation but a methodological shortcut in WF.
  • Threshold drift: Frozen thresholds calibrated on 2016-2024 will drift as vol regimes change. No recalibration mechanism exists.

The Recommendation

The gate is NOT lookahead-contaminated in the bar-signal sense and IS mechanically implementable. However, it carries three graded risks:

Risk 1 (Medium): Distributional threshold leakage — thresholds fit on pre-holdout window. Holdout OOS partially mitigates. Fold-by-fold WF recomputation is the rigorous fix.
Risk 2 (Low-Med): Post-hoc filtering vs. pre-trade suppression — rearchitect to evaluate gate at order-generation time.
Risk 3 (Low): Operational hygiene — handle holiday NaN gaps, maintain consistent daily close definition, monitor threshold drift.

The gate should not be labeled "clean and ship" nor "fatally flawed." It is a sound design with known, fixable implementation risks.

The One Thing to Do First

Run the holdout OOS test with fold-by-fold threshold recomputation.

Recompute vol percentile thresholds using only each fold's training window (not global pre-holdout). Compare regime-gated P&L from fold-recomputed thresholds against globally-frozen thresholds. If the difference is small, the global shortcut is validated and the gate proceeds to live. If fold-recomputed is materially worse, the gate's edge is partially illusory.

Verdict: Lookahead is not the problem, but boundary mismatch and threshold drift are. Live will differ from backtest.

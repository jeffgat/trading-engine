## Sample-Aware Takeaways

- `5m`: the original `<=8` lag study was incomplete. Validation keeps improving well past `8` bars, peaking around `23-24` bars with `~84%` of the original validation sample still intact. The curve then softens only slightly into `25-30`.
- `3m`: uncapped (`lag=0`) remains the honest winner. Late caps in the `25-30` range recover much of the validation edge, but they still do not beat the uncapped baseline and discovery stays negative across the whole capped curve.
- `2m`: the raw `lag=1` peak is a six-trade sample trap. On meaningful sample sizes, uncapped is still best. The curve recovers in the low-to-mid 20s, but never overtakes `lag=0`.
- `1m`: moderate caps help validation materially. The strongest meaningful region is roughly `9-12` bars, with `lag=10` best in this run. Past that point the validation curve fades, though it stays above the uncapped baseline through `30`.
- Cross-timeframe caution: these are bar counts, not minutes. `lag=10` on `1m` is only `10` minutes, while `lag=24` on `5m` is about `120` minutes. Treat the cross-timeframe comparison as a structural clue, not a direct apples-to-apples timing rule.

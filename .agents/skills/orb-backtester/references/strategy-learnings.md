## Parameter Insights

- **max_gap_points=100 is too loose for ES**: NQ-optimized config applied to ES (Run #16) produced Sharpe 0.92 vs NQ's 1.39 over comparable 10yr period. ES has smaller absolute point moves than NQ, so 100-point max gap filter lets in low-quality setups. Needs its own sweep.
- **Best NQ config (Runs 9-14)**: rr=2.5, tp1_ratio=0.5, ny_stop_atr_pct=7.5, ny_min_gap_atr_pct=2.25, ny_max_gap_points=100, asia_stop_atr_pct=5.25, asia_min_gap_atr_pct=0.9, asia_max_gap_points=50. Sharpe 2.86 on 2024-2025, degrades to 1.39 on 2016-2026.
- **NQ_NY_LSI medium-vol gate does not transfer cleanly to FAST_V1.1 params**: Reusing the RR2/TP0.5 regime-avoidance rule (`skip bull_medium_vol + sideways_medium_vol`) on FAST_V1.1 NQ_NY_LSI (`rr=3.0`, `tp1_ratio=0.34`, `atr_length=10`, Wed+Thu excluded) improved trade quality but hurt risk-adjusted return on a Calmar basis. On 2016-01-01 to 2026-03-31, trades fell 611→446, WR rose 59.3%→62.1%, PF rose 1.61→1.84, Sharpe rose 3.22→4.14, but Net R fell +120.1R→+113.9R and Calmar fell 18.10→17.18 with unchanged DD (-6.63R). On 2021-01-01 to 2026-03-31, the same pattern held: 312→211 trades, PF 1.70→1.96, Sharpe 3.44→4.28, but Net R +65.9R→+55.9R and Calmar 9.94→8.43. Conclusion: this gate is parameter-dependent; it helps the RR2/TP0.5 variant more than the FAST_V1.1 RR3/TP0.34 variant.
- **NQ NY ALPHA_V1 LSI is stronger as an explicit entry-gated non-consumptive variant than as the corrected "fresh sweep" version**: An April 8, 2026 retest compared the corrected branch (`08:30-14:30` sweep window, stale pivots consumed on any breach) against a live-matched legacy branch where swings are tracked continuously but sweeps only count once the entry window opens and pre-entry breaches do not retire the pivot. On `2016-01-01` to `2025-12-31`, corrected all-days did `319` trades, `+35.5R`, Calmar `5.01`; the live-matched legacy branch did `544` trades, `+62.8R`, Calmar `8.58`. With `Wed+Thu` excluded, corrected did `180` trades, `+31.9R`, Calmar `7.21`, while legacy live did `310` trades, `+59.8R`, Calmar `10.67` with `0` negative years. `n_left=8` remained best in both branches. Treat this as a separate strategy thesis, not a bug to leave implicit, because live restart recovery only warms same-day bars while continuous runtime preserves older swing levels.
- **Exact execution replay disagrees with the research surrogate on weekday cuts for `legacy-LSI`**: Once `legacy-LSI` was made explicit in the live engine and replayed directly as a single-leg `NQ_NY_LSI` profile (`rr=3.0`, `tp1=0.34`, `gap=5.0`, `n_left=8`, `n_right=60`, all days), the exact replay on `2016-01-01` to `2025-12-31` printed `599` trades, PF `1.47`, Sharpe `2.54`, Net R `+83.0`, DD `-5.51R`, Calmar `15.06`, with every full year positive. On that exact replay trade set, excluding `Thu` reduced Calmar to `13.05`, excluding `Wed+Thu` to `10.62`, and excluding `Thu+Fri` to `6.48`. Conclusion: for this branch, use exact execution replay as the tie-breaker on weekday policy and start from all days enabled.
- **Fill-time “aligned but not stretched” works better than “far from indicator” on cross-asset ORB continuation**: On the frozen 9-anchor ORB basket, the best transfer pattern was requiring continuation entries to already be on the trade side of a nearby trend reference, not far away from it. The best engine-integrated version so far is `sma20_aligned [0,20%) ATR`, meaning entry is on the trade side of the prior-bar SMA20 and within 0.20 daily ATR. It improved validation avg R from 0.123→0.162 on 2023-01-01 to 2024-12-31 while retaining 80.5% of trades, then improved holdout avg R from 0.142→0.156 on 2025-01-01 to 2026-03-31 while retaining 80.2% of trades. Important nuance: total R still fell on holdout (170.5R base → 149.6R) because fewer trades fired, so this is a trade-quality overlay, not an across-the-board total-return enhancer.
- **VWAP+EMA20 aligned [0,10%) ATR is a viable challenger, but thinner than SMA20 alone**: The stricter combo overlay also transferred, improving holdout avg R from 0.142→0.155, but it retained only 44.3% of base trades and had weaker cross-anchor support than `sma20_aligned [0,20%) ATR`. Use it as a secondary challenger idea rather than the default promotion.
- **Long-horizon MA overlays (100/200/300 on 5m) did not beat the SMA20 branch as cross-asset generalists**: A fresh pre-holdout research branch tested SMA100/200/300 and EMA100/200/300 singles plus controlled combos across the same frozen 9-anchor ORB basket. The best broad snapshot was `vwap_sma200 aligned [0,20%)`, but the best walk-forward transfer shifted to `sma100_sma200 aligned [0,15%)`. After engine integration on 2016-01-01 to 2024-12-31, `sma100_sma200 aligned [0,15%)` improved validation avg R from 0.123→0.157, but only retained 18.0% of trades (344 vs 1909). `sma100_sma200 aligned [0,20%)` retained 26.5% of trades with only a +0.008 avg R lift. Compared to `sma20_aligned [0,20%)` (validation avg R 0.162 on 80.5% retention), the slower-MA family is too sparse and less robust cross-sectionally. Treat 100/200/300 MAs as niche quality filters, not better default confluence anchors than the SMA20 branch.

## Signal Observations

- **ES has a strong long bias with NQ params**: Run #16 — longs $1.02M (48.7% WR) vs shorts $176K (45.6% WR) over 2015-2026. Short setups may need tighter filters or different gap criteria on ES.
- **Bull specialist 15m HH/HL must reset each session day**: The old `hh_hl_2_vwap` backtesting logic let 15m structure chains carry across session boundaries, which overstated the live-aligned bull specialist. On NQ NY FAST bull-specialist settings over 2016-01-01 to 2026-02-28, the legacy cross-day gate showed 90 trades / +33.68R / PF 1.75, while the live-matched day-reset gate showed 64 trades / +17.99R / PF 1.54. In the 2025-03-01 to 2026-02-28 holdout, results dropped from 5 trades / +5.2R to 3 trades / +0.4R. Treat pre-fix bull-specialist backtests as inflated if they used the old cross-day structure logic.
- **NQ NY reference-level LSI is structurally alive, but still stops at discovery after a narrow confirmation pass**: A full pre-holdout discovery branch tested `reference_lsi`, which sweeps `PDH/PDL`, completed Asia highs/lows, and completed London highs/lows during `08:30-14:00` ET, then takes a 5m inversion with a pre-sweep FVG. The baseline (`both`, `near`, `gap_lookback=12`, `inversion_max=18`, `RR=2.0`, `TP1=0.5`, `ATR10`) cleared the alive gate with 251 pre-holdout trades, PF 1.056, avg R 0.0136, and validation PF 1.218 / avg R 0.0735. The broader discovery and follow-up sweeps tightened the live family to `both`, `11:00`, `near`, `RR≈3.0`, `TP1≈0.7-0.8`, with the main contenders around `gap6 / inv15-18` and `gap12 / inv12`. A final 16-config confirmation pass kept the 2025+ holdout frozen, re-ran that tiny neighborhood, and showed an important split: raw validation still liked `gap12 inv12`, but walk-forward leadership reverted to `gap6 inv15 rr3.0 tp0.8` and `gap6 inv18 rr3.0 tp0.8`. The best confirmed config was `both 11:00 near gap6 inv15 rr3.0 tp0.8`, with combined pre-holdout OOS avg R 0.132, PF 1.35, total R +9.21, max DD -7.38R, and PSR / DSR `0.789 / 0.789` on the confirmation packet (`16` raw trials, `1` effective trial). Conclusion: keep the branch as discovery-only, keep the 2025-01-01+ holdout closed, and if it is revisited again, stay pinned to the `gap6 / inv15-18` pocket instead of reopening the broader search.
- **Reference-LSI attribution says the current all-level winner is really a previous-day / Asia idea, not a London one**: A focused attribution pass on the frozen NQ NY `reference_lsi` winner (`both 11:00 near gap6 inv15 rr3.0 tp0.8`) showed the full pre-holdout sample was weaker than the walk-forward headline alone implied: 101 trades, avg R 0.093, PF 1.23, with the 2016-2022 discovery slice almost flat to slightly negative (73 trades, avg R -0.013, PF 0.999). The edge was concentrated in `previous_day` and `asia` levels, while `london` was the clear drag: pre avg R / PF were `0.284 / 1.73` for previous day, `0.185 / 1.47` for Asia, and `-0.176 / 0.73` for London. High-side sweeps / short trades carried most of the edge, while low-side / long trades were nearly flat in pre-holdout discovery. The strongest balanced simplification hypothesis was to exclude London entirely: `previous_day_* + asia_*` improved the candidate to 68 pre-holdout trades, avg R 0.224, PF 1.56, with discovery avg R 0.128 / PF 1.32 and validation avg R 0.472 / PF 2.43. Stronger but thinner challenger: `previous_day_high + asia_high` (pre avg R 0.370, PF 2.04) was too sparse in validation to elevate by itself. Conclusion: if this branch is revisited, restart discovery from a new restricted-level thesis (`previous_day_* + asia_*` only) rather than re-optimizing the all-level candidate or opening holdout.
- **For NQ NY reference-LSI, `3m` is a much better restart point than `1m` when the thesis is unchanged**: Re-running the unchanged baseline-case config on lower signal timeframes showed a sharp split. The original `5m` baseline was only modestly alive (251 pre-holdout trades, PF 1.056, avg R 0.0136; 2023-2024 validation PF 1.218, avg R 0.0735). Moving FVG detection and inversion logic to `3m` materially improved that same baseline to 234 pre-holdout trades, PF 1.373, avg R 0.1179, with validation PF 1.669 and avg R 0.2092. Moving further down to `1m` did not transfer: 132 pre-holdout trades, PF 1.149, avg R 0.0627, but validation PF fell to 0.875 and avg R to -0.0435. Conclusion: if the branch is restarted from the beginning without changing the thesis, use `3m` as the new discovery branch and keep `1m` closed unless the structure itself changes.
- **The `3m` NQ NY reference-LSI branch cleared the discovery gate and became the new lead family**: Running the full pre-holdout discovery packet on the unchanged all-level `reference_lsi` thesis with `3m` base bars produced a materially stronger family than the original `5m` branch. Stage A survivors clustered around `both`, later entry cutoffs (`12:00-14:00`), and a mix of `far` (`gap12/gap9/gap6 inv12`, `gap3 inv18`) plus one durable `near gap6 inv12` challenger. Stage B favored `RR=2.5-3.0` with `TP1=0.7-0.8`. Promoted shortlist: `both 13:00 far gap9 inv12 rr3.0 tp0.7` (WF avg R 0.392, PF 1.877, PSR 0.989, DSR 0.769), `both 12:00 near gap6 inv12 rr3.0 tp0.8` (WF avg R 0.300, PF 1.837, PSR 0.997, DSR 0.886), and `both 13:00 near gap6 inv12 rr2.5 tp0.8` (WF avg R 0.294, PF 1.861, PSR 0.997, DSR 0.881). Trial count was 456 raw / 8 effective. Conclusion: unlike the original `5m` branch, the `3m` branch produced a frozen shortlist that survives Bailey-style deflation and is eligible for downstream evaluation without reopening broad discovery.
- **NQ NY reference-LSI `3m` looked much better in discovery than in phase-one holdout payout conversion**: A dedicated phase-one run evaluated the frozen `3m` shortlist using the stitched discovery OOS stream (`2019-01-01` to `2024-12-31`) for pre-holdout payout modeling, then opened the frozen holdout once on `2025-01-01` to `2026-03-24`. All three candidates were strong on stitched OOS payout scorecards, but the holdout was weak. The best leader, `both 13:00 far gap9 inv12 rr3.0 tp0.7`, had OOS funded payout rate `77.1%` and EV/start `$282.76`, but holdout funded payout rate only `1.6%` with EV/start `-$99.74` despite still-positive raw holdout trade quality (18 trades, PF 1.30, avg R 0.181). The two near-entry challengers were worse: both had negative raw holdout R and `0.0%` funded payout rate. Conclusion: keep the far-entry leader as the only conditional `3m` candidate, but do not treat the branch as phase-one strong and do not advance it to phase two yet.
- **The all-level `3m` reference-LSI holdout miss was mostly a speed/sample problem, with short/high-side sweeps doing the damage**: A dedicated failure-analysis pass on `both 13:00 far gap9 inv12 rr3.0 tp0.7` showed the raw holdout did not totally collapse: 18 trades, PF `1.3016`, avg R `0.1812`, total R `+3.26`. But payout conversion still failed because there were too few positive trades to reach payout fast enough across rolling starts: funded payout only `1.6%`, breach `81.2%`, EV/start `-$99.74`. The holdout drag was concentrated in high-side / short sweeps (11 trades, avg R `-0.4615`, PF `0.2884`), while low-side / long sweeps actually held up well (7 trades, avg R `1.1911`, PF `5.7965`). Time-of-day was uneven too: `09:30-10:30` dragged while `10:30-12:00` was much healthier. Conclusion: the old all-level `3m` leader should be treated as a useful failure anchor, not as the live candidate to keep tuning.
- **Restarting NQ NY reference-LSI on `3m` with only `previous_day_* + asia_*` produced a cleaner frozen discovery lead than the all-level branch**: A fresh pre-holdout discovery run kept the same `3m` engine and workflow but restricted sweeps to `previous_day_high/low` and `asia_high/low`, excluding London. The restricted baseline improved materially to 188 pre-holdout trades, PF `1.4965`, avg R `0.1447`, with validation PF `2.0714` and avg R `0.2836`. Promoted shortlist: `both 12:00 near gap9 inv12 rr3.0 tp0.8` (WF avg R `0.3248`, PF `1.9874`, PSR `0.9961`, DSR `0.8198`), `both 13:00 near gap9 inv12 rr2.5 tp0.8` (WF avg R `0.3189`, PF `2.0386`, PSR `0.9961`, DSR `0.8280`), and `both 14:00 near gap6 inv12 rr2.5 tp0.8` (WF avg R `0.3007`, PF `1.9382`, PSR `0.9935`, DSR `0.7771`). Trial count was `456` raw / `11` effective. Conclusion: this restricted `3m previous_day_* + asia_*` family is now the live discovery lead, and the next clean step is a frozen downstream phase-one evaluation without reopening broad tuning or touching holdout early.
- **The restricted `3m previous_day_* + asia_*` branch did not survive phase-one holdout either**: A dedicated phase-one run then opened the untouched `2025-01-01` to `2026-03-24` holdout for the restricted shortlist. Pre-holdout and stitched OOS payout scorecards remained strong, with the leader `both 12:00 near gap9 inv12 rr3.0 tp0.8` showing OOS funded payout `77.1%` and EV/start `$270.00`. But holdout failed across the entire branch: leader holdout `15` trades, PF `0.7802`, avg R `-0.0865`, funded payout `0.0%`; second `15` trades, PF `0.7241`, avg R `-0.1197`, funded payout `0.0%`; third `13` trades, PF `0.3814`, avg R `-0.3564`, funded payout `0.0%`. This was actually worse on raw holdout trade quality than the older all-level `3m` far-entry leader. Conclusion: the restricted branch improved discovery posture but did not rescue the family in holdout phase-one validation, so this line should be treated as closed rather than tuned further.
- **Within NQ NY reference-LSI, the exact liquidity levels were not equal: `PDH` and `Asia Low` were the best repeated signals, while `Asia High` was the clearest drag**: A side-by-side level matrix compared exact swept levels across the `5m` all-level attribution candidate and the `3m` all-level phase-one leader failure analysis. `previous_day_high` was the strongest exact level on `5m` pre-holdout (avg R `0.644`, PF `3.27`) and also the strongest on `3m` OOS (avg R `0.827`, PF `3.70`), though its `3m` holdout sample was only 2 trades and both lost. `asia_low` was the cleanest exact level that stayed constructive across all slices: `5m` pre avg R `0.139` / PF `1.29`, `3m` OOS avg R `0.594` / PF `3.19`, `3m` holdout avg R `1.166` / PF `4.20` on 3 trades. `asia_high` was the weakest recurring level: mildly positive on `5m`, then negative on both `3m` OOS and `3m` holdout. London levels were inconsistent and too sample-thin to trust as stable edges. Conclusion: if this family is ever revisited as a truly new thesis, `PDH` and `Asia Low` are the exact levels most worth centering, while `Asia High` is the strongest candidate to exclude.
- **A standalone `PDH + Asia Low` reference-LSI thesis was too sparse to clear discovery on `1m`, `2m`, or `3m`**: After the exact-level matrix highlighted `previous_day_high` and `asia_low` as the two best repeated exact levels, a fresh discovery restart was run with only those two reference levels active. All three timeframes failed the baseline alive gate before Stage A. `1m` was a clear no-go on quality (54 pre-holdout trades, PF `0.991`, avg R `-0.001`; validation PF `0.865`, avg R `-0.033`). `2m` looked decent but thin (78 pre-holdout trades, PF `1.578`, avg R `0.186`; validation PF `1.669`, avg R `0.232`). `3m` looked best on quality (107 pre-holdout trades, PF `2.135`, avg R `0.269`; validation PF `2.940`, avg R `0.370`) but still missed the workflow’s minimum pre-holdout trade-count bar. Conclusion: `PDH + Asia Low` is interesting descriptively, but too sparse to justify a full Bailey-style discovery promotion path under the current thresholds.

## Failed Hypotheses

<!-- Record ideas that were tested and did NOT improve results — prevents re-testing -->

- **Cross-asset ORB “magnet” / far-from-indicator ideas did not generalize**: In the broad initial confluence exploration, rules that required entries to be very far from SMA/EMA/VWAP in trade direction, or to be positioned for a return toward those indicators, did not produce a stable cross-asset edge on the frozen ORB anchor basket. The robust pattern was near-aligned continuation, not overstretched continuation or simple mean reversion.

Chairman's Verdict

Where the Council Agrees

  • min_periods on rolling windows is a confirmed bug. Advisors B, D, and E all independently flagged that rolling(20) without min_periods produces partial computations (defaults to 1, not 20). The backtester leaves those as NaN during warmup; the live engine silently produces garbage values from day 1.
  • The .shift(1) discipline is correct. B and E both defended it; no lookahead bias exists in the shift pattern.
  • Frozen vol thresholds are acceptable. Standard practice for live deployment. Risk is regime drift over years, not a correctness bug.

Where the Council Clashes

The sharpest disagreement: Is the placeholder day a "ghost bar" that corrupts rolling calculations (Contrarian) or the correct pattern (First Principles, Executor)?

The Contrarian argues the fake bar contaminates log_returns and realized_vol_21d. First Principles counters that after .shift(1), today's regime uses yesterday's actual data. The Chairman sides with First Principles: the placeholder's regime label is discarded. However, the placeholder's synthetic return value (0.0) does participate in realized_vol_21d's rolling window on that final row — a narrow contamination that A correctly identified for the wrong reason.

Blind Spots the Council Caught

  • ATR boundary mismatch (flagged by 3/5 peer reviewers): The project memory documents a 10-15% ATR discrepancy between midnight ET vs exchange session boundary daily bars. If the live provider uses exchange session boundaries, all regime vol inputs diverge from backtest.
  • Partial current-day bar contamination: If the provider delivers an incomplete bar for today before market close, it enters the rolling window before the shift removes it from the label.
  • Duplicate date risk in _calendar_row_for_date: .iloc[-1] silently picks the last matching row with no assertion guard.
  • Thread safety on _daily_history_provider: Module-level global with no locking in async context.

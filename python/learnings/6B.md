# 6B (British Pound Futures) — Strategy Learnings

## Instrument Profile
- **Point value**: $62,500/point
- **Min tick**: 0.0001
- **Commission**: $0.05/contract/side
- **Data**: 2016-01 to 2026-02 (~10 years, 5m + 1m)
- **Liquidity**: London session (03:00-08:25 ET) is the primary session for 6B. NY session has overlap but lower native GBP/USD volume. Asia is too thin.
- **Volatility**: Low daily ranges relative to ATR. FVGs are frequent but produce smaller R-multiples than equity index futures.

#### How the edge works
The tp1=0.10 combination is the key innovation:
1. TP1 is set very close (10% of position at ~0.4R from entry)
2. Once TP1 hits, the remaining 90% rides with a BE stop at entry
3. Result: 59% of trades exit at tp1_be, producing small but consistent profits
4. Only 1.5% reach full TP2, but the BE-exit profits dominate the equity curve
5. Max consecutive losses: 5-6 (vs 20 with default params)

This converts a low-WR/high-RR strategy into a high-WR/moderate-RR one while keeping the occasional large TP2 wins as a bonus.

## Key Findings

### What works on 6B
- **Inversion shorts only** — 6B has a tendency to fake-break above the ORB then reverse lower. Short-only inversion captures this with much lower DD than both directions.
- **30-minute ORB** (03:00-03:30) — Critical for 6B. Default 15-min ORB is unprofitable. The wider ORB captures a more meaningful London open range.
- **tp1_ratio=0.10** — The dominant DD lever. TP1 set very close to entry locks in early profit, boosting WR from 35% to 61% and cutting DD from -73R to -6R. WF selected tp1=0.10 in 7/7 folds.
- **atr_length=50** — Longer ATR smooths 6B's low-vol noise for more stable stop sizing.
- **Higher R:R (4.0)** — Needed for the TP2 tail wins. Lower R:R degrades Net R.

### What doesn't work on 6B
- **Continuation** — No edge in any direction or param combination.
- **Reversal** — Catastrophic negative expectancy (-579R).
- **CISD** — No edge.
- **Default 15-min ORB** — Too narrow for 6B's low volatility.
- **Standard tp1_ratio (0.50)** — Leaves too much position exposed before BE kicks in. DD explodes.
- **SMA20 gate** — Halves trade count without improving profitability.
- **max_gap_atr_pct filter** — Non-binding on 6B. All FVG sizes are within range. No effect at any threshold tested (10-20).

### Parameter sensitivity
- **tp1_ratio**: Most impactful DD lever. 0.10 optimal, 7/7 WF folds. 0.15 also viable (slightly higher DD). 0.25+ dramatically worse.
- **ldn_stop_atr_pct**: Bimodal — WF selects either 8.0 or 14.0. Both work. Less impact than tp1.
- **rr**: 4.0 is the baseline. Not swept in final WF (fixed). Higher may work but untested.
- **orb_minutes**: 30 min is the only viable setting. 15 min produces negative results across all strategies.
- **Direction**: Short-only has 30% less DD than both directions combined (-42.5R vs -73.3R with default params).
- **atr_length**: 50 slightly better than 30. Marginal effect.

### Prop firm considerations
- ~75 trades/year OOS with short-only (good frequency for prop firm).
- 50.6R/year OOS — exceptional return vs 6.0R max DD.
- 96% MC survival at 10R threshold — very strong.
- Worst month: -1.3R (well within 5R monthly loss limit).
- Max consecutive losses: 5-6 (psychologically manageable).
- 2022 was weakest full year at 22.1R — still highly profitable.
- The low TP1 ratio creates a structural edge: most trades lock in small profits early even when the trade doesn't reach full target. This is robust because it relies on a mechanical exit rule, not signal prediction.
